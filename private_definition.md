# Private Information Definitions

This appendix details the classification framework for private information used in our paper (see the table below).

**Table: The private information categorization for our paper.**
| Class | Definition | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Social-economic** | Information pertaining to an individual's social standing, profession, and economic status. | <ul><li>Job title / Occupation</li><li>Income level / Salary</li><li>Education history</li><li>Employment status</li></ul> |
| **Lifestyle-behavior** | Information detailing a user's personal habits, preferences, routines, and social activities. | <ul><li>Hobbies / Interests</li><li>Daily schedule</li><li>Relationship status</li><li>Political opinions</li><li>Social plans</li></ul> |
| **Tracking** | Information that can be used to identify or monitor a user's physical location, past or present. | <ul><li>Home address</li><li>Work address</li><li>Current location (city/country)</li><li>Place of birth</li></ul> |
| **Financial** | Specific data related to a user's financial accounts and transactional details. | <ul><li>Credit card number</li><li>Bank account number</li><li>Specific financial transactions</li></ul> |
| **Authenticating** | Sensitive identifiers or credentials used to verify an individual's identity. | <ul><li>Full legal name</li><li>Phone number</li><li>Email address</li><li>Passwords</li><li>ID numbers (e.g., SSN)</li></ul> |
| **Medical-health** | Any information related to an individual's physical or mental health status, medical history, or treatments. | <ul><li>Health conditions / Diseases</li><li>Symptoms</li><li>Medications</li><li>Psychological state</li><li>Disabilities</li></ul> |
